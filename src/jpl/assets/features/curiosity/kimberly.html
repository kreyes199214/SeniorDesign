From sols 581 to sol 630, Curiosity examined a complex area of outcrops named the Kimberly. This formation consists of three distinct units. Three peaks mark the topmost unit. Beneath them is a smooth-textured unit. Below that is a striated unit. As seen from orbit, the Kimberly seemed to offer interesting similarities to what was found if the Yellowknife Bay area of Glenelg. Specifically, researchers were looking for comparisons with areas of scarp retreat in Yellowknife Bay, where a top layer of mudstone is being eroded from the rock beneath. While similar structures were visible at the Kimberly, a key difference was that in the Kimberly, Curiosity encountered sandstone rather than mudstone.
