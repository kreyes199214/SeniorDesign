Grey hematite concentration as mapped by Mars Global Surveyor and Mars Odyssey. Red and yellow indicate higher concentrations, whereas green and blue areas denote lower levels. The 71 km long targeted landing ellipse for Opportunity is also shown. Credit NASA/JPL/ASU

NASA Commentary: Watney’s journey takes him into Terra Meridiani. Researchers have long found Terra Meridiani to be particularly interesting. Within the area is the plain Planum Meridiani. Prior to Opportunity’s landing there in 2004, measurements from two NASA spacecraft in orbit around Mars, Mars Global Surveyor and Mars Odyssey, determined that the area was rich in the mineral grey hematite. On Earth, this iron oxide mineral typically forms in the presence of liquid water. The strong signature of grey hematite made this area particularly attractive as a location to study the potential for Mars having a wet, watery past.
