Endeavour crater as seen by Opportunity rover

A long journey brought Opportunity to the edge of Endeavour Crater. At 22 kilometers in diameter and 300 meters deep, it is by far the largest crater that Opportunity will examine. During its exploration along the rim of the crater, Opportunity came upon a vein of calcium sulfate, probably in the form of gypsum, protruding up above the surrounding rock.

Color view of the mineral vein called "Homestake" from Opportunity's panoramic camera.

This vein, named Homestake, probably formed from water dissolving calcium out of volcanic rocks and then combined with sulfur either leached from the rock or from volcanic gas. This deposit tells a story of water that was far less acidic (and possibly more hospitable to life) than was found in previous locations.
