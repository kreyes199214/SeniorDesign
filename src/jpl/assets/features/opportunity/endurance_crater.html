Endurance crater as seen by Opportunity rover

After leaving Eagle crater, scientists at NASAâ€™s Jet Propulsion Laboratory directed Opportunity on a journey that took it to Endurance crater (130 meter diameter, 20 meter depth). Upon arrival at the crater, it was obvious that the crater exposed layers of stratified rock. A survey conducted from the rim found a safe entry point from which to drive Opportunity into the crater. Study of the layers of exposed rock showed the complexity of the history of water in this area with the region having apparently been flooded on multiple occasions, with dry periods in between. At the floor of Endurance Crater is a field of sand dunes, which the rover team chose to avoid so as to minimize the risk of getting the rover stuck. After spending over six months inside Endurance Crater, Opportunity exited safely and continued its journey across Meridiani Planum.
