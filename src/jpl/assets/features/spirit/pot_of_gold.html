A rock that researchers named "Pot of Gold"

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory directed Spirit toward the Columbia Hills, a range of low hills on the floor of Gusev crater, about 3 kilometers from Spirit's landing site. These hills are composed of older material rising above the volcanic debris surrounding it. As Spirit climbed into the hills, the mineralogy around it changed. A rock that researchers named "Pot of Gold" was found to contain the mineral hematite, which often forms in the presence of water.
